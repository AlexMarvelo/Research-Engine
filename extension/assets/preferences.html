About the WorldBrain project

WorldBrain is an open-source project that aims to battle (scientific) misinformation by developing a quality score for web content/sources and an expert ranking for users/authors/scientists in a given field. Our first step is a personalisable search-engine that lets users full-text searches on the pages they visited and bookmarked.
In later stages users can voluntarily share metadata about their browsing behaviour with WorldBrain, from which we develop the quality scores. Also this will make it possible to develop a crowdsourced search-engine where we can collectively show each other related content. (i.e. counter perspectives or unsourced studies)
You can read more about it in our project pitch deck.

Transparency & Data Ownership

We have no access to your data and won't ever have without your prior approval. Your data truly stays yours.
We will publicly explain who benefits in what way from the analysis on the data users voluntarily share. Be it the user himself, the community or the project's income streams. You can read more about it in our project pitch deck.
